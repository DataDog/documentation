<div id="cdoc-content" class=""><article><h2 id="overview">Overview</h2><p>Frustration signals identify points of user friction in your mobile application by capturing patterns like rage taps and error taps.</p><p>Mobile RUM collects two types of frustration signals:</p><dl><dt>Rage Taps</dt><dd>A user taps on an element more than three times in a one-second sliding window within a radius of approximately 9 mm (48 dp).</dd><dt>Error Taps</dt><dd>A user taps on an element, and at least one error occurs during the action's duration or within 100 ms after the action ends.</dd></dl><p>The Mobile RUM SDK automatically detects frustration signals with no additional configuration.</p><h2 id="search-for-frustration-signals-in-the-rum-explorer">Search for frustration signals in the RUM Explorer</h2><p>Frustration signals appear in the <a href="/real_user_monitoring/explorer/">RUM Explorer</a> as action attributes. Search through your RUM data to surface trends on frustration signals and analyze patterns of user friction.</p><p>Enter a facet in the search query to begin. Available search fields include:</p><dl><dt>Frustration Type</dt><dd>Find actions with a specific frustration signal. For example, to see all actions with a rage tap, add <code>action.frustration.type:rage_tap</code> to the search query.</dd><dt>Frustration Count</dt><dd>Find sessions and views where any frustration signal occurred. For example, to find sessions or views with at least one frustration signal, add <code>session.frustration.count:&gt;1</code> or <code>view.frustration.count:&gt;1</code> to the search query.</dd></dl><h2 id="further-reading">Further reading</h2><div class="whatsnext"><p>Additional helpful documentation, links, and articles<!-- -->:</p><ul class="list-group"><a
